Rajma

Rajma is a popular North Indian dish made with red kidney beans. The beans are cooked in a thick, flavorful gravy made from onions, tomatos, ginger, garlic, and a variety of spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. It's typically served with rice (known as "Rajma Chawal") and is loved for its rich taste and comforting texture. Rajma is a good source of protein and is commonly enjoyed as a vegetarian meal acroos India.

Ingredients

  • Rajma (Kidney Beans) - 1 cup (soaked overnight)
  • Onions - 2 medium, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, pureed or finely chopped
  • Garlic - 4 to 5 cloves, minced
  • Green chillies - 1 to 2, finely chopped (optional)
  • Cumin seeds - 1 to 2
  • Bay leaf - 1 to 2
  • Turmeric Powder - Half teaspoon
  • Coriander powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder - Half teaspoon
  • Garam Masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - 2 tablespoon
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves) - for garnish
  • Water - for cooking the beans and making the gravy
  1. Soak and Cook the Rajma :- Wash and soak 1 cup of rajma in water overnight or 6-8 hours. Then drain the soaked beans and pressure cook with 3-4 cups of water and a pinch of salt for 15-20 minutes (4-5 whistles),until soft.
  2. Prepare the Masala :- Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil. Then add the ingredients teaspoon cumin seeds, bay leaves, onions, ginger, garlic cloves, green chilies, tomatoes in the amount as directed in the ingrediengts. The add turmeric powder, coriander powder, teaspoon cumin powder, red chili powder, and salt to taste. Then cook for a minute.
  3. Combine Rajma and Masala :- Add the cooked rajmas to the masala, then simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. Mash a few rajma beans to thicken the gravy, then add 1 teaspoon garam masala and stir well.
  4. Garnish and Serve :- Turn off the heat and garnish with freshly chooped cilantro. Serve hot with stemed rice or roti.

Enjoy!!!
